Question 7: Density
On a scale from 0 to 1, what is the density of this network? Please round off your responses to 2 decimal places.

  • 0 means an empty graph with no link.
  • 1 means a fully connected graph.
Tip: Please use your responses from Question 1 and Question 2 to compute density.

The correct answer is: 0.13.

The density of a network is a measure of how many links between nodes exist compared to how many links between nodes are possible.

From Question 2, we know there are 12 links exist in this network.

From Question 1, we know there are 10 nodes exist in this network, and hence 10 × (10 - 1) total links possible.

To calculate density, we compute by 12 ÷ (10 × 9) ≅ 0.13.
